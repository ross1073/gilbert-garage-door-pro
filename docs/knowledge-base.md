## Services We Offer

### Garage Door Repair
General repair for all types of garage doors. Common problems include broken springs, opener failures, off-track doors, worn cables, damaged panels, noisy operation, sensor issues, and doors that won't close completely.

### Garage Door Opener Repair
We fix all opener brands and types. Common problems: motor burnout, stripped gears, remote/keypad not working, wall button failure, chain or belt slipping, circuit board failure. Arizona heat is especially hard on opener motors — garages can exceed 140°F in summer, causing thermal overload and premature failure. We service chain drive, belt drive, screw drive, and wall-mount/jackshaft openers.

### Garage Door Spring Repair
We replace both torsion and extension springs. Torsion springs mount above the door on a steel tube. Extension springs run along the horizontal tracks. Warning signs of spring failure: loud bang, door won't open or feels very heavy, visible gap in spring coils, door opens unevenly, cables hanging loose. **Spring repair is dangerous — never attempt DIY.** Springs are under extreme tension and can cause serious injury. Standard springs last about 10,000 cycles (7-10 years). Arizona heat shortens this lifespan. Most Gilbert homes were built around 2001, meaning original springs are well past their expected life. We recommend replacing both springs at the same time.

Specialized service for all LiftMaster models:
- **Belt drive (8550W, 8500W):** Ultra-quiet, premium residential

### Garage Door Installation
We install new garage doors and openers. Door types: sectional (most common), carriage house, modern/contemporary, roll-up. Materials: steel (most popular), aluminum, wood, composite/faux wood. Insulation matters in Arizona — an R-12 to R-18 door reduces garage temps by 20-30°F in summer. We recommend polyurethane insulation over polystyrene. Smart opener upgrades available with LiftMaster myQ for phone control and battery backup.

### Garage Door Sensor Repair
Safety sensors are mounted 6 inches above the floor on each side of the door opening. One sends an infrared beam, the other receives it. If the beam is broken, the door reverses. Required by federal law since 1993.

Sensor LED guide:
- Sending eye: steady amber/yellow = powered and working
- Receiving eye: steady green = aligned properly
- Receiving eye: blinking/red = misaligned

Common problems: misalignment (bumped by foot or bike), dirty lens, sun interference (very common in Gilbert — west-facing garages get direct afternoon sun that overwhelms the sensor), wiring damage, sensor failure.

Simple troubleshooting: check if LEDs are lit, clean lenses, check alignment, look for obstructions, check wiring. If these don't fix it, call us.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How quickly can you get to my home?**
Same-day service throughout Gilbert and the East Valley. Most calls, we arrive within 1-2 hours. Emergencies are prioritized.

**Do you repair all garage door brands?**
Yes. LiftMaster, Chamberlain, Genie, Craftsman, Linear, Wayne Dalton, Clopay, Amarr, and all others.

**Is it safe to repair a garage door spring myself?**
No. Garage door springs are under extreme tension and can cause serious injury or death. Always call a professional.

**Do you charge extra for emergency/after-hours service?**
No. Our repair pricing is the same regardless of when you call. No extra fees for evenings, weekends, or holidays.

**Do you offer free estimates?**
Yes. We provide free on-site estimates for all services. You know the exact cost before any work begins.

**Should I repair or replace my garage door opener?**
If your opener is over 15 years old, lacks modern safety features, or needs frequent repairs, replacement is usually more cost-effective. Modern openers are quieter, safer, and offer smart home connectivity.

**Should I replace both springs at the same time?**
Yes. If one spring breaks, the other is near the end of its life too. Replacing both saves a second service call and ensures balanced operation.

**Why does my opener stop working in summer?**
Arizona heat causes motors to overheat, especially in uninsulated garages. The thermal overload protector shuts the motor off to prevent damage. Let it cool 15-30 minutes. If it happens repeatedly, the motor may need service.

**Why do my sensors stop working in the afternoon?**
Very common in Arizona. West-facing garages get direct afternoon sun that overwhelms the infrared beam. Solutions include sensor hoods/shields or repositioning sensors.

**What should I do if my garage door is stuck open?**
Do not force it. Pull the red emergency release cord to disconnect the opener. Call us immediately — we're available 24/7.
